Mars Industries has expanded by adding a third Detroit location. Our new 8 acre facility located on Strecker Street in Southeast Detroit, will serve as a home for our transportation department's equipment of trucks, rolloffs and in-plant hoppers. In addition, Mars' truck maintenance group will also relocate to this facility. Centralization of these assets will continue our increased efficiencies in logistics for our scrap customers.
 
2004 ended with a downward push from scrap prices that reached record highs.
The dollars decline, a potential "soft landing" for China's economy and worldwide steel demand are just a few of the global factors that continue to effect increasingly volatile U.S. scrap prices.
At publication, 1st quarter 2005 numbers are still being established.
Contact your Mars Industries representative for current market information.
 
 
In September, 2004, John Suchon, Vice President of Business Development for Mars Industries, was presented the General Motors "2004 We Care Award" by GM's Worldwide Facilities Group. The awards were given out at a ceremony that took place at the General Motors Technical Center in Warren, MI.
 
 
Dennis Russell formerly with Marketing Partners LLC, has joined Mars Industries in the role of Director of the Steel Division.
Dennis' group will be trading new steel and oversee Mars Steel Service Center.

Previous to working at Marketing Partners, Dennis had been with Acme Steel as their Senior Vice President of Sales and Marketing and National Steel as its Regional Director.
